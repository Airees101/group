Malwares & Viruses

Malware is a software with the intention of causing harm and can be done through viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, or adware, with these just to name a few

The Types of Virus Definition
Viruses The basic definiton that most people use is virus, but in reality there are actually different types, some even with different effects. For general terms, a virus is a program created for malicious intentions of infecting one or spreading to many computers to disrupt the network. They can get into your computer either through downloaded files, email links, or just by going through the internet.
Worms A worm does not need to attach to a program to run it's virus, and these can replicate themselves and work to their full ability without any user interaction. Scary right? Your computer just needs to be on and you could be getting attacked while you don't have anything open.
Trojans Trojans act just like the ancient story with the trojan horse, where they act and seem like a normal program but are actually carrying a harmful payload that can cause some damage to your computer. However, these are usually only for one computer and don't necessarily spread to other computers.
Backdoor Trojans Same as a regular trojan but these allow attackers to gain access to your computer from the comfort of their own. Allowing them to access your computer as if they were sitting right next to you and accessing all your information.
